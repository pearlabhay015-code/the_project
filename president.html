Role as Visitor at CUSB
Constitutional Responsibilities

The President of India serves as the Visitor of all Central Universities by virtue of their constitutional office. As Visitor, Smt. Murmu is the apex authority overseeing the Central University of South Bihar, holding ultimate powers in matters of governance, appointments, and institutional oversight.

  • Apex constitutional authority over all Central Universities
  • Appoints the Chancellor on the advice of the government
  • Holds residual powers in matters of university governance
  • Represents the highest national authority for CUSB

Public Service & Legacy
Milestones & Contributions

Smt. Murmu has devoted her life to empowering marginalised communities and tribal populations across India. Her journey from a small Odisha village to Rashtrapati Bhavan stands as an inspiring symbol of India's democratic strength and inclusive progress.

  • First woman Governor of Jharkhand (a tribal-majority state)
  • Won Pandit Nilkanth Das Best Legislator Award — 2007
  • Championed tribal rights, education & constitutional values
  • Introduced exam & recruitment reforms in Jharkhand state universities

Career Journey

Public Service & Political Career

A remarkable journey spanning government service, legislative leadership, and India's highest office.

🇮🇳
2022 – Present
President of India · Visitor, All Central Universities
Sworn in as the 15th President of India on 25 July 2022. As Visitor, she is the constitutional apex authority over CUSB and all other Central Universities.
🏛️
2015 – 2021
Governor of Jharkhand
First woman tribal Governor of Jharkhand, a tribal-majority state. Earned praise from leaders across party lines for statesmanship and adherence to democratic values. Introduced reforms in university examination and recruitment processes.
🗳️
2000 – 2009
Member of Legislative Assembly (2 Terms)
Elected from Rairangpur constituency, Odisha. Served as Minister of State (Independent Charge) for Commerce & Transport (2000–2002) and Fisheries & Animal Resources Development (2002–2004). Won Best Legislator Award in 2007.
📚
1994 – 1997
Honorary Teacher
Served as an honorary teacher at Sri Aurobindo Integral Education Centre, Rairangpur — demonstrating her lifelong commitment to education.
💼
1979 – 1983
Junior Assistant, Government of Odisha
Served as a Junior Assistant in the Irrigation and Power Department, Government of Odisha — the beginning of her distinguished career in public service.
